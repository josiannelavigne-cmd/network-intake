## Product Purpose

A client-facing UX deliverable produced by BYCo for Bell Canada's Centrak 2.0 redesign. Documents the TPM's future state workflow across 5 phases, bridging the completed discovery phase and the wireframe phase now beginning (Feature 1 — Intake Integration). The map is the reference artifact for review: stakeholders use it to verify scope, flag open questions, and sign off on direction before BYCo moves into screen design. A version of this map already exists in the Figma file; this is the polished, production-quality replacement.

## Brand Personality

Simple. Professional. Polished.

The work should feel considered and calm — not a pitch deck, not a workshop output, not a template filled in. It should look like someone who knows exactly what they're doing made it with care.

## Anti-references

- Generic agency slide decks (clip art icons, gradient banners, stock-photo aesthetics)
- Busy consulting reports (dense prose, too many visual layers, competing emphasis)
- Miro / FigJam sticky-note boards (informal, low-fidelity, workshop feel)
- Cookie-cutter UX deliverable templates (the kind anyone could generate in 10 minutes)

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity before completeness** — if a stakeholder has to work to understand the structure, the structure has failed. Layout and hierarchy carry meaning before words do.
2. **Earned detail** — every visual element must serve a function. No decorative borders, no gradient accents, no color for color's sake.
3. **Brand without noise** — BYCO tokens provide the palette; restraint provides the quality. One accent role per semantic meaning, used consistently.
4. **Scannable architecture** — a reader should be able to locate any phase, any row, any status at a glance. The grid is the navigation.
5. **Internal confidence** — this is review quality, not presentation theater. No inflation. Clean, direct, done.
6. **Open means open** — the "Still Open" row carries real unresolved design questions, not placeholders. The visual treatment (amber territory) must communicate genuine uncertainty — these are live decision points for Mo and Aveena.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Internal review quality. No formal WCAG compliance required, but text must be legible at standard Figma zoom levels and color must not be the sole carrier of meaning (status dots pair with labels).
